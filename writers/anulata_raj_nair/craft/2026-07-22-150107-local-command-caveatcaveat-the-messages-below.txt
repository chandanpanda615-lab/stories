  The strongest thing in Ajnabi

  She gives the reversal an object instead of a speech.

  For three parts you convict Ammi as the mother who walked out. Then Safia
  lands her cruellest line — "Aapko zara bhi afsos nahi hai na apne kiye ka"
  (You feel no regret at all, do you?) — and Ammi doesn't defend herself. She
  leaves the room with:

  ▎ "Daal jal rahi hai shayad, main abhi aayi"
  ▎ The dal's probably burning. I'll be back.

  Safia follows and finds her on the floor with a small suitcase: a cloth doll
  cut from her own Banarasi saree, a plastic camera from a fair, a crocheted
  frock, Safia's first drawing. Thirteen years of evidence assembled by someone
  who never expected an audience.

  You overturn your own verdict. Nobody argues you out of it. And that dal line
  reads as evasion on first hearing, as unbearable composure ten seconds later.

  Act on it: when you need a reader to reverse on a character, don't let that
  character testify. Put the proof in an object they made with no expectation of
  ever being believed.

  Three more worth taking

  - The diary earns three jobs across four parts — a wound in Part 1 ("June 2004
  — Dear Ammi, I hate you"), a map in Part 2 (Ammi's address on the last page),
  a letterbox in Part 4 (Arhaan's note). One object, three functions, beats
  three objects with one each.
  - She weaponises the serial break. Part 1 ends on "vah aurat ammi ke siwa aur 
  koi nahi thi" — that woman was no one but Ammi. Part 2 opens: "vah ammi nahi 
  thi" — it wasn't her. Your drop proves Safia's longing before Safia will admit
  to it.

  sometimes the wounds a scalpel can't lance will split open at the touch of a
